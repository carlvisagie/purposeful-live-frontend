# Purposeful Live Coaching - Regulatory, Compliance & Risk Framework

**Version:** 1.0 | **Status:** Strategic Blueprint | **Scope:** Global Compliance

## Executive Summary

Purposeful Live Coaching operates in highly regulated industries (healthcare, insurance, financial services). This framework ensures compliance with all applicable regulations while enabling rapid growth across markets.

## Healthcare Compliance

### HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)

**Scope:** All US operations handling protected health information (PHI)

**Key Requirements:**
- Encrypt all PHI at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Implement access controls and audit logging
- Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with all partners
- Breach notification procedures (60 days)
- Patient rights (access, amendment, deletion)
- Minimum necessary principle for data access

**Implementation:**
- Dedicated HIPAA compliance officer
- Annual HIPAA training for all staff
- Regular security audits and penetration testing
- Business continuity and disaster recovery plans
- Incident response procedures
- Breach notification system

**Timeline:** Achieve HIPAA compliance by end of Year 1

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

**Scope:** All EU operations and any EU resident data

**Key Requirements:**
- Data minimization (collect only necessary data)
- Purpose limitation (use data only for stated purposes)
- Consent management (explicit opt-in)
- Data subject rights (access, rectification, erasure, portability)
- Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs)
- Data Protection Officer (DPO)
- Privacy by design and default

**Implementation:**
- Dedicated GDPR compliance officer
- Privacy policy and terms of service
- Consent management system
- Data subject request procedures
- DPIA for high-risk processing
- DPO appointment
- EU data residency options

**Timeline:** Achieve GDPR compliance by end of Year 1

### FDA (Food and Drug Administration)

**Scope:** Any medical devices, diagnostic tools, or health claims

**Key Requirements:**
- Device classification (Class I, II, or III)
- 510(k) premarket notification (if applicable)
- Quality management system (QMS)
- Clinical evidence for health claims
- Adverse event reporting
- Post-market surveillance

**Implementation:**
- Regulatory affairs team
- Clinical evidence collection
- Quality management system
- Adverse event tracking
- Regular FDA monitoring

**Timeline:** Determine FDA requirements by end of Year 1, implement as needed

### State Medical Board Regulations

**Scope:** Coaching and medical advice by state

**Key Requirements:**
- Coaching vs. medical advice distinction
- Telemedicine licensing requirements
- Prescriptive authority limitations
- Informed consent procedures
- Medical record retention

**Implementation:**
- Legal review by state
- Coaching protocols (non-medical)
- Clear disclaimers
- Telemedicine compliance by state
- Medical record management

**Timeline:** Achieve state compliance by end of Year 2

## Data Privacy & Security

### Data Classification

**Public Data:** Non-sensitive information (marketing content, public profiles)
**Internal Data:** Employee and operational information
**Confidential Data:** Business-sensitive information (financial, strategic)
**Restricted Data:** PHI, PII, financial information (highest protection)

### Data Handling Procedures

**Collection:** Minimize collection, explicit consent, clear purpose
**Storage:** Encrypt at rest, secure access controls, regular backups
**Processing:** Purpose limitation, data minimization, secure processing
**Sharing:** BAAs/DPAs with partners, no unauthorized sharing
**Retention:** Delete when no longer needed, comply with legal holds
**Deletion:** Secure deletion, audit trail, verification

### Access Controls

**Authentication:** Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all users
**Authorization:** Role-based access control (RBAC), principle of least privilege
**Audit Logging:** Log all access, review logs regularly
**Segregation:** Separate development, staging, production environments
**Encryption:** Encrypt sensitive data, key management

### Incident Response

**Detection:** Monitoring systems, user reports, security testing
**Response:** Incident team activation, containment, eradication
**Notification:** Notify affected users, regulators (as required), within legal timeframe
**Recovery:** Restore systems, verify integrity, post-incident review
**Documentation:** Document all incidents, lessons learned, improvements

## Risk Management Framework

### Risk Categories

**Regulatory Risk:** Non-compliance with laws and regulations
**Operational Risk:** System failures, data breaches, service disruptions
**Financial Risk:** Revenue loss, cost overruns, funding challenges
**Market Risk:** Competition, market shifts, adoption challenges
**Reputational Risk:** Negative publicity, loss of trust, brand damage
**Legal Risk:** Lawsuits, liability, contract disputes
**Talent Risk:** Key person dependencies, recruitment challenges
**Technology Risk:** Cybersecurity, system scalability, technical debt

### Risk Assessment Matrix

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|-----------|
| Data breach | Medium | High | Encryption, monitoring, incident response |
| Regulatory non-compliance | Low | High | Compliance team, regular audits, legal review |
| System outage | Low | High | Redundancy, monitoring, disaster recovery |
| Key person departure | Medium | Medium | Documentation, cross-training, retention |
| Competitive threat | Medium | Medium | Innovation, differentiation, partnerships |
| Funding shortfall | Low | High | Diversified revenue, cost management |
| Reputational damage | Low | High | Quality focus, transparency, crisis management |

### Risk Mitigation Strategies
